Tuesday, 1 November 2016

Close win for Room 19!
Room 19 of Ngaio School, have claimed their first C.A.C victory over Room 11. This Catch A Cone game took place on the Ngaio School field on Monday the 31st October. This match was a very hard one with both teams ending with 3 cones. It came down to the fair play points. Room 19 had all their fair play points awarding them another 10. This meant they got 25 points. Room 11 had only 9 fair play points. Aidan C of Room 19 was shoved to the ground by Elliot F of Room 11. Aidan received a big graze to the knee and arm; Elliot was shown a yellow card for his foul play. This gave Room 11 a total score of 24. The final score: 25 points (Room 19) to 24 points (Room 11.) After the match, Room 11 was claiming an incident happening on the Room 19 side of the pitch. Joshua C was accused of being tagged and not sitting down even though he denied it. Room 19 joined in on the argument that Room 11’s teacher, Dan B was referee and was biasing. “The game was a tough but good one and we had a team plan and we stuck to that,” said Aidan C of Room 19. Zach E said after the match: “It all came down to one thing: the fair play points, I was shivering when the Referee, Dan Byrne read out the final points.”Zach E said, also of  Room 19. Room 19 are looking forward to playing against the other year 5 class, Room 10.

Reporter: Oliver Hays.
