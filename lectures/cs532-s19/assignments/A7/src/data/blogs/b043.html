Somewhere Only I Know

In a moment the world can change.
Life is exceptionally hard. Every new day is a challenge. And I have it better than most people. My life has been paved with helping hands lifting me up where I want to be. I have moments of extreme anxiety where I can hardly breathe. My heart speeds ahead of me, and so I hold my chest. Every other decision and thought is pushed out of my mind.
Deep breaths.
There's always tomorrow. Things are going to be hard. Life is hard. The struggle is never going to stop. Change is never going to take the day off. Challenges won't stop appearing.
It was enough, in this moment, to have a glimmer of happiness.
A chance, however hard it would be, to grow.
In my head I decided that if I was afforded this chance, I would hit the ground running. I would push and pull, and work hard.
It was both terrifying and exciting, all in one gulp.
But it was here.
And I would do it.

"Now it's 16 miles to the promise land. And I promise you I'm doing the best I can. Now some days last longer than others. But this day, by the way, went too fast."

~ Arms Outstretched by Rilo Kiley

Sunday, February 5, 2017

Hearts

I used to feel like this when I played Spoons.
You know, the game where you try to find matches, and when you do, you take a spoon? It's like musical chairs, but 100X worse. I would always get super anxious. I didn't want to lose. It pushed me to succeed, but I'm also sure took years off my life.
So when it returns, I know what it is.
I know what it feels like.
My heart is racing, threatening to burst out of my chest.
My stomach is turning, performing unique acrobatics.
My head hurts.
I try to breathe slow. Let the anxiety wash over me completely so it can wash away. It's like being trapped in a current. Sometimes you have to let go and stop fighting it and wait for it the swirls to pull you back up on the other side where you can breathe.
I find myself unconsciously holding my breath, and unable to control my thoughts. I decide to just try and make them stop. Halt them before they go where I don't want them to.
I want to fall asleep and not think again.
I shouldn't be surprised. And I'm not surprised. But that doesn't stop me from drowning.

Monday, December 12, 2016

Control

1, 2, 3, stop.
Slow down slow down slow down.
Be purposeful.
I'm running this race. It's one of those times I feel my movements coming unglued. Out of control. This is what gets people into accidents. This is what leaves people mangled.
The real athletes learn to control their muscles. Even when you're tired, everything has to be tight. Planned and intentional. And it's hard to do that. So you have to train. You have to push your body to move a certain way.
Maybe it's more like gymnastics or a dance. Part of the art is complete control of movement. Beauty in specificity.
Deep breath and.....
Hold.
Practice patience.
Practice control.
Practice management.
And then, be zen.
Work with the current but don't be defined by it.
Love everyone but don't be controlled by them.
And most of all, just be.

Wednesday, November 30, 2016

Neon

Do you ever wonder about the impermanence of a sunset?
The colors are so striking, so loud. Setting everything aflame. Unfortunately, the flame is due to burn out. In five minutes time, the same hillside turns dull. The moment of beauty is gone.
Yet, do we ever doubt the sunset will come back?
Moments of beauty come in knowable intervals.
I felt vibrant.
The edges of me tingled and buzzed. My heart exploded in ribbons of technicolor feelings. I felt alive. I felt awake.
Something about this was different. Something was begging to be noticed and heard, but not overwhelming me with fear and doubt. I felt empowered, not weighted. I didn't have to change who I was.
Something about that was so beautiful. And yet, it was so sad.
I was awake for a moment, dimmed in the next. Loud and bold turned to quiet and cold.
Surely this feeling wasn't temporary. Surely it was possible to feel this way again. This couldn't be the only time in my life I felt awakened and light, see the clarity in things.
My paradigms shifted like Jenga blocks.
Don't feel stupid, I thought. Don't apologize. Don't ever think it's wrong to be bold, and bright, and loud, and dramatic. That's just how you are. Let this moment inform you. Let the beauty of someone else alight you.
Don't stop.



"She's always buzzing just like
Neon, neon
Neon, neon
Who knows how long, how long, how long
She can go before she burns away"

~ Neon by John Mayer

Tuesday, October 25, 2016

Women

"You were meant for something more,"
a tiny voice inside me quietly thrummed.
Life is more than this.
"What would you do if you were not afraid?"
She throws her notebook on the floor.
The image strikes me as loud as the thwack with which it hits the floor.
What would I do?
I'd find another job.
Or go to graduate school.
Or admit that I'm an amazing advisor and there's no shame to that. Even as I feel like I'm standing, dripping in a mold of femininity.
I would stop worrying about love and dating and marriage and kids.
I am ready to move.
Take my opportunities.
"Fortune favors the bold."

Sunday, August 28, 2016

Bayou

Some days I feel great.
Everything falls into place, I work hard, I play hard. My life feels like it's on a track. I know where I'm going.
Today isn't one of those days.
All of a sudden something simple fills me with rage. Not a good sign, I think. Before I know it, I blossom like a helium balloon. There's heat in my cheeks. Why am I so mad? I try to set it down, put it away. I place my palms down, breathing slowly. Go away, my brain begs.
I go out. Somewhere and with people I feel safe. At least I thought. It's like the world conspires against me.
Every once in a while, I think, not so quietly, I hate everyone.
I don't. But I'm surprised how often the thought surfaces. Why is this all I get? Creepy men following me to book club.
I try to swallow the thoughts back.
But they're unruly.
For some reason they brings thoughts of other things. Suddenly I'm mad. I'm mad at you for being disingenuous. I'm mad for you calling me babe. I'm mad for you wrapping your arms around me. I'm mad for you buying me popsicles. I'm mad for you pulling me in to dance. I'm mad for you talking about my birthday like you'll still be here.
None of it is real!
This thought has hit me in the gut, slapped me in the face, knocked me to the ground.
It doesn't mean ANYTHING.
It's not real. You're not real.
I watch the difference in your language change from "love" to "care" and my insides wrench.
You're not real.
Run, I think.
You should run.
I can't help a look back with a stumble.
It's not real. None of it was ever real.

Friday, August 26, 2016

Desert

For the first time I'm filled with apathy.
So what? bouncing off the walls of my skull.
So what?
It's both a revelation and a let down. I look at the road ahead of me, It's lonely. I have a compulsion to quit. To phone a friend in this unknown wilderness. That's not a choice. My feet crunch and the dulcet tones calm and silence me.
I spend a lot of time looking at the ground, as is my habit on hikes like this. Eyes trained on scrubby, gray-brown dirt flecked with pebbles. I look up, and I'm still just as far away as I was before.
Is this how it's going to be?
So what? my brain answers.
This is how it is.
Is that the sun setting?
So what?
It would be nice, I think, to be wrapped up quietly in bed. It would be nice, I think, to have someone there. Lots of things would be nice.
So what?
Like running into a tree, oof. I can't take those so whats anymore.
Alright I think. There's nothing else for it. I just have to keep moving forward. This can't last forever. This place has virtues of its own. The light is pretty, slanting like that, orange and radiant. Casting gold on all it touches. Even the quiet is pleasant every once in a while, the crunch of my shoes. This place is ok. And it won't last forever.  I might even miss the winding, crunchy grass and speckled pebbles. Sometimes desolation is its own art, sometimes it's not desolation at all.
Shut down the parts I don't want to listen to, and just hear the crunch.
Just see the sky.
Just feel the breath of wind.
Pull the curls from my face.
It's going to be ok.
This can't last forever.
Even if I wanted it to.
